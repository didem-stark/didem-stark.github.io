Dr. Didem Stark

Junior Group Leader | Hertie Institute for AI in Brain Health

University of Tübingen, Germany | Walter Benjamin Fellow (DFG)

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, bias and fairness, and clinical neuroimaging data. Supported by the prestigious Walter Benjamin funding programme (DFG), I focus on identifying hidden biases that disadvantage minority groups in medical applications, analyzing how debiasing methods perform across diverse subpopulations, and establishing rigorous evidence classification schemes to transition clinical AI from fairness findings to robust fairness claims.

As AI integrates deeper into clinical pipelines, my objective is to establish robust, ethical frameworks addressing evidentiary claims, model interpretability, and algorithmic disparities, ensuring these powerful tools benefit all members of society equally.

Research Focus

Algorithmic Fairness

Developing sequential evidence-classification schemes and rigorous validation frameworks to transition clinical AI from preliminary fairness findings to robust fairness claims.

Neuroimaging & ML

Applying complex deep learning techniques safely to structural MRI and clinical datasets for neurology and psychiatry applications.

Ethical AI in Medicine

Ensuring model interpretability, evidence validation, and equitable treatment constraints before machine learning deployment.

Consulting & Advisory Services

I am open to consulting on data science, trustworthy AI, and clinical deployment readiness.

I help organizations address the practical realities of deploying machine learning in sensitive healthcare spaces—ensuring that validation cohorts match deployment populations, bias audits target real clinical risks, and models maintain robust performance across diverse subpopulations.
