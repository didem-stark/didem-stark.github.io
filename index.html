Dr. Didem Stark

Postdoctoral Researcher | Hertie Institute for AI in Brain Health

University of Tübingen, Germany

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, bias and fairness, and clinical neuroimaging data. Supported by the prestigious Walter Benjamin funding programme (DFG), I focus on identifying hidden biases that disadvantage minority groups in medical applications, analyzing how debiasing methods perform across diverse subpopulations, and investigating what persistent model misclassifications and disease heterogeneity reveal about neurological disorders like Alzheimer's.

As AI integrates deeper into clinical pipelines, my objective is to establish robust, ethical frameworks addressing disease heterogeneity and algorithmic disparities, ensuring these powerful tools benefit all members of society equally.
